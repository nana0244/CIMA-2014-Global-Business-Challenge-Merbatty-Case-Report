## 📌 Project Overview

This project is a strategic business case analysis submitted for the CIMA 2014 Global Business Challenge. The team analysed a fictional luxury boat manufacturing company, **Merbatty**, operating across Europe, the USA, and the Middle East. The report identifies and prioritises key business issues and provides actionable recommendations across strategy, ethics, finance, and operations.

---

## 🌍 Industry Context

- Global recreational boat market valued at **$23.9 billion (2008)**, projected to reach **$25.8 billion by 2014**
- Market leaders concentrated in the **UK, Italy, France, and the USA**
- Growing competition from **Asia and Australia** in standard boat segments
- Demand shift towards **larger, luxury, and mega yachts**, especially in Europe and the Middle East

## 📦 Issue 1 — Late Hull Delivery (Topcrest)

**Problem:** Topcrest postponed hull delivery by 2 months, threatening a customer's delivery deadline.

**Financial Analysis:**

| | Amount (€) |
|---|---|
| Idle labour cost to Merbatty | €53,078 |
| Total compensation offered by Topcrest | €143,464 |
| Customer compensation included | €25,000 |

**Options Assessed:**
- Accept Topcrest's offer ✅ *(recommended — net positive, relationship preserved)*
- Switch to secondary supplier ❌ *(€50,000 additional cost, still late, no compensation)*

**Recommendations:**
1. Accept Topcrest's deal (short term)
2. Build relationships with alternative suppliers (long term)
3. Consider **backward integration** — acquire or develop in-house hull production capability

---

## 🤝 Issue 2 — JKL Shareholder Concerns

**Problem:** JKL (28% shareholder) raised concerns about charitable spending and family influence in decision-making.

**Key Discussion Points:**
- CSR activities (charity secondments rising from €3m to €8m) offer brand and reputational benefits — but create cash flow pressure
- Family interference (Jesper lobbying his father for speed boat approval) undermines professional governance
- Merbatty is now a **listed company** — decisions must be made in shareholders' best interests

**Recommendations:**
- Embed professionalism, integrity, and objectivity into all decision-making
- Develop clear policies separating family interests from board decisions
- Improve shareholder communication and transparency

## 🎨 Issue 3 — Designer Selection (Surania Facility)

**Shortlisted Candidates:** Cooper Designs vs. Arabian Interiors

| Criteria | Cooper Designs | Arabian Interiors |
|---|---|---|
| Experience | Extensive, award-winning internationally | 4 years, Middle East only |
| Cost per design | €100,000 | €90,000 |
| Technology | CAD + Extranet for customer collaboration | CAD only |
| Market reach | Global | Middle East focused |
| Capacity | Confirmed global readiness | Dependent on Sheikh's approval |

**Recommendation:** Select **Cooper Designs** — superior quality, technology, and capacity despite slightly higher cost.

## ⚠️ Issue 4 — Accident at Work (Ethics)

**Problem:** An employee (Paulo) was found using drugs at work. The manager (Jesper) failed to report it.

**Ethical Analysis (multiple frameworks applied):**

| Framework | Conclusion |
|---|---|
| Utilitarianism | Report — protects the majority |
| Caring ethics | Report — to access rehabilitation support |
| Justice principle | Report — fairness and impartiality required |
| Rights & duties | Caution — reporting may infringe individual rights |

**Recommendations:**
- Adopt a **Just Culture System** — encourages reporting without fear of punishment
- Implement **Employee Assistance Programs** (confidential support channels)
- Consider periodic drug testing (with full legal guidance)
- Make the workplace environment more positive and engaging

---

## 👔 Issue 5 — Sales Staff vs. Sales Agents

**Debate:** Should Merbatty replace agents with permanent employed sales staff?

| Factor | Sales Agents | Permanent Staff |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | Commission only (4%) | Fixed salaries + office overheads |
| Relationships | Long-established customer contacts | Need time to build |
| Control | Less management complexity | More complex to manage across 6 continents |
| Industry norm | Standard practice in luxury boats | Non-standard |

**Recommendation:** Retain current sales agents + **increase commission incentives** to drive larger boat sales.

---

## 🚤 Issue 6 — Speed Boat Diversification

**Proposal:** Jesper proposed building and racing a speed boat as a market diversification play.

**Break-Even Analysis:**
- Fixed costs estimated at **€38.6 million**
- Break-even point: **21 units** per year
- Additional costs: €8m build cost + €5m existing sponsorship = **€13m upfront**

**Assessment (SAF Framework):**

| Dimension | Finding |
|---|---|
| Suitability | Aligned with Merbatty's capabilities and new model strategy |
| Feasibility | High cash strain, no guaranteed returns |
| Acceptability | High risk — Jesper has no racing experience; reputational exposure |

**Recommendation:** Place proposal **on hold** until the 5-year plan is achieved.
